Computer Science Grad to SCADA Programmer

Published: 2025

Graduating with a degree in Computer Science doesn't mean you're locked into traditional software engineering roles. In fact, the industrial automation world desperately needs people who can bridge the gap between traditional control systems and modern software solutions.

My journey from CS graduate to SCADA Developer opened doors in a niche but rapidly growing industry — Industrial IoT (IIoT). Understanding programming fundamentals gave me an advantage when working with PLCs, building MQTT infrastructures, deploying cloud SCADA systems, and scripting KPIs across field assets.

Today, industries are hungry for engineers who understand networking, scripting, and modern cloud-native principles — all skills taught in CS programs, but often underutilized in manufacturing and energy sectors.

For those looking for an alternative to traditional software careers, industrial automation is a goldmine of opportunity.
